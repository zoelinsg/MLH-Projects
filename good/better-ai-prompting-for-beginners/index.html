✨🤖Better AI Prompting for Beginners

Learn how to write clearer prompts, give better specs, and handle images safely.

What Makes a Good Prompt? 💡

A good prompt helps the AI understand exactly what you want. It usually includes four key parts:

  • A clear goal: What should the AI do? (e.g., "Summarize", "Explain", "Rewrite").
  • Enough context: Background information or examples so the AI has what it needs.
  • Constraints: Limits like length, tone, or items to avoid.
  • Expected output format: Specify bullets, paragraphs, steps, code, or JSON.

Short example: "Summarize the key benefits of remote work for new managers in 4 bullet points."

Simple Prompt Formula ➕

Use this quick formula to build clear prompts:

Goal + Context + Constraints + Output Format

Write each part as a short phrase. Then combine them into one sentence or short paragraph.

Example:
Goal: "Explain photosynthesis" — Context: "for high school students" — Constraints: "3 sentences" — Output: "bullet points" →
"Explain photosynthesis for high school students in 3 sentences as bullet points."

Better Specifications 📝

Good specifications tell the AI everything it needs to meet your expectations. Be explicit about these points:

  • Who the content is for: Age, background, or role (e.g., "beginner", "manager").
  • What features are needed: Any specific items or parts to include.
  • What technology to use: If you need code, name the language or tool (e.g., "Python", "HTML").
  • What style is wanted: Tone, formality, or voice (friendly, formal, concise).
  • What should be avoided: Words, formats, or assumptions not allowed.

Example spec: "Create a short tutorial for beginners that uses plain language, includes one code example in Python, and avoids advanced math terms."

Safe Image Upload Tips 🔒

Images can reveal private information. Follow these safety steps before uploading:

  • Do not upload sensitive personal information: Avoid photos of IDs, passports, or private documents.
  • Avoid IDs and account details: Do not share addresses, phone numbers, passwords, or account screens.
  • Check the photo background: Look for hidden private information (post-its, mail, screens) and crop or blur it out.
  • Be explicit about the task: Tell the AI exactly what to do with the image (describe, analyze, redact), and what it must not do.
  • Read the tool's privacy policy: Know how images are stored, used, or shared by the service.

Common Mistakes Beginners Make ⚠️

  • Prompts are too vague: Vague requests don't give the AI a clear direction.
  • No goal is given: If you don't state the goal, the AI may guess incorrectly.
  • Too many requests at once: Split complex tasks into separate steps.
  • No output format specified: Ask for bullets, paragraphs, steps, or code to get consistent results.
  • Trusting AI without checking: Always verify facts, sources, and outputs before using them.
